# Screenplay Contests & Fellowship Programs

The difference between a contest and a fellowship: **contests give you credibility and money**. Fellowships give you **a job, a career, and industry relationships**. Do both. Understand which is which.

---

## TIER 1 — Industry Gold

Placing in these changes your life. Agents and managers actively monitor these lists.

---

### The Academy Nicholl Fellowships in Screenwriting
**The gold standard. Nothing else comes close.**

- **Sponsor:** Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (the Oscars people)
- **Prize:** Up to 5 fellowships per year at $35,000 each
- **Entry fee:** ~$45
- **Deadline:** Typically late April / early May (check academy.com/nicholl)
- **What they want:** Feature screenplays in any genre. They're looking for distinctive voices, not formula.
- **What winning actually gets you:** Your name circulates through every major management company and production company in Hollywood. You will get phone calls you could never get on your own. Past fellows have gone on to write for major studios, win Oscars, and launch decades-long careers.
- **The quarterfinalist bump:** Even placing in the top 10% (quarterfinalist) is worth putting in your query letters. It signals to the industry that your work passed professional vetting.
- **Strategy:** Submit your best, most personal script. They don't want the thing written to sell. They want the thing only you could write.

---

### Austin Film Festival Screenplay Competition
**Best for writers who want to make real industry connections.**

- **Prize:** ~$5,000 for feature winner + industry meetings + conference passes
- **Entry fee:** ~$40-55 depending on category
- **Deadline:** May/June (early), August (final)
- **Conference:** October in Austin — the real prize
- **What makes Austin different:** The conference brings together working writers, showrunners, managers, and development executives in an environment where doors are actually open. The panels are world-class. The after-parties are where careers start.
- **Categories:** Feature Drama, Feature Comedy, TV Drama Pilot, TV Comedy Pilot, Short Film
- **What winning/placing gets you:** Industry cred + conference access + pitching opportunities. Many working writers say this is where they made their first real Hollywood contact.
- **Strategy:** Enter your best feature AND your best pilot. The TV categories are less competitive and the industry hunger for pilot specs is real.

---

### Final Draft Big Break Contest
**Strongest relationship with the agency and management community.**

- **Prizes:** Grand prize $35,000 + meetings. Category winners $1,000 + meetings.
- **Entry fee:** ~$60
- **Deadline:** June/July (check finaldraft.com/big-break)
- **Categories:** Action/Adventure, Comedy, Drama, Horror/Thriller, Sci-Fi/Fantasy, and more
- **What makes it different:** Final Draft is the industry-standard software and has deep relationships with CAA, WME, UTA, and major management companies. Winners get introductions.
- **The meetings:** They actually happen. Winners have turned Big Break into representation and option deals.

## TIER 2 — Solid Industry Signal

These won't change your life on their own, but they build your credibility stack. Agents do scan these lists. Winning multiple Tier 2 contests starts to look like a pattern.

---

### ScreenCraft
- **What it is:** A suite of genre-specific contests (Action, Drama, Horror, Sci-Fi, Thriller, Comedy, etc.)
- **Prize:** Varies by category ($1,000-$3,000) + industry introductions
- **Entry fee:** ~$50-70
- **Deadline:** Multiple deadlines throughout year
- **Why it matters:** Genre-specific wins look great in query letters to genre-focused producers. A ScreenCraft Horror win when querying Blumhouse is noticed.
- **Website:** screencraft.org

---

### WeScreenplay
- **What it is:** Feature and TV competitions with an emphasis on diverse voices
- **Prize:** Cash + industry meetings
- **Entry fee:** ~$40-55
- **Notable:** Their feedback notes are actually useful if you pay for coverage. Good for getting a professional read on what's working.
- **Website:** wescreenplay.com

---

### PAGE International Screenplay Awards
- **What it is:** One of the longer-running contests with strong industry ties
- **Categories:** 10+ genre categories
- **Prize:** Gold Prize $25,000. Multiple category prizes.
- **Entry fee:** ~$45-65
- **Deadline:** April/May
- **Gold/Silver/Bronze:** Even a Bronze in your category is usable in a query letter
- **Website:** pageawards.com

---

### BlueCat Screenplay Competition
- **What it is:** Known for providing written feedback with every entry — rare and valuable
- **Prize:** $10,000 Grand Prize
- **Entry fee:** ~$55
- **Why writers love it:** Even if you don't place, you get notes. That feedback is worth the entry fee alone if you're early in your writing development.
- **Website:** bluecatscreenplay.com

---

### Shore Scripts
- **What it is:** UK-based but strong international reach
- **Prize:** Feature grand prize £5,000
- **Entry fee:** ~$40
- **Why enter:** Growing reputation, international co-production lens, good for writers whose stories have a global angle
- **Website:** shorescripts.com

---

## TIER 3 — Useful for Practice, Minimal Industry Signal

These are fine to enter if you're early in development or want to see how your script does in competition. Don't burn significant time building your pitch around a win here.

- TrackingB Amateur Weekend (actually better than Tier 3 — industry tracks this one)
- Script Pipeline
- Scriptapalooza
- Genre Screenplay Contest
- Various film festival associated contests

## FELLOWSHIP PROGRAMS — Career-Makers

These are not contests. They are job programs. Winning a fellowship means you get **paid to write** for a major studio or network, mentored by working professionals, and entered into their hiring pipeline for years.

---

### Academy Nicholl (see above — it's both)

---

### Disney/ABC Writing Program
- **What it is:** One year paid fellowship to write for ABC shows
- **Focus:** TV drama and comedy
- **Prize:** Paid writing position + mentorship + agent/manager introductions
- **Application:** Annual — typically open in spring
- **What it leads to:** Disney/ABC staffing pipeline for years after. Many current showrunners started here.
- **Requirements:** Original pilot spec + existing show spec. Strong diverse voices especially encouraged.

---

### CBS Writers Mentoring Program
- **Focus:** TV comedy and drama
- **Structure:** 9-month program with mentorship from working CBS writers
- **Application:** Typically open fall/winter
- **What it leads to:** Direct pipeline into CBS network shows

---

### NBC/Universal Writers on the Verge
- **Focus:** TV writers
- **Structure:** 3-month intensive with Saturday workshops
- **What they want:** Emerging writers with distinct voice and 1-2 completed specs

---

### Warner Bros. Television Writing Workshop
- **Focus:** TV drama and comedy
- **What it is:** 12-week workshop leading to potential staff writer positions
- **Application:** Fall cycle, typically. wbwritingworkshop.com
- **Most competitive:** ~4,000 applicants for ~8 spots. But those 8 spots lead directly to WB show staffing.

---

### The Black List Feature Lab
- **What it is:** Hosted by The Black List platform, focused on features
- **Structure:** Writers selected from Black List database receive mentorship from established screenwriters
- **How to qualify:** Have your script evaluated on The Black List platform (blcklst.com) and score well

---

### Sundance Screenwriters Lab
- **What it is:** Prestigious five-day intensive lab for feature film writers
- **Structure:** One-on-one sessions with Creative Advisors (established filmmakers)
- **Focus:** Independent, director-driven features — not commercial genre
- **Application:** Fall. Project must be director-attached or have a clear path to production.

## The Strategic Contest Plan

Don't spray and pray. Here's the sequence:

**Year 1 (Script Development Phase)**
- Enter BlueCat for the feedback notes
- Enter WeScreenplay to test the market response
- Use the notes to rewrite

**Year 2 (Credibility Building Phase)**
- Enter Nicholl (always)
- Enter Austin Film Festival (always)
- Enter the relevant ScreenCraft genre contest
- Enter PAGE

**If you place anywhere in Year 2:**
- Lead with that placement in every query letter
- Apply to the fellowship programs that match your format (TV vs feature)
- Update your Black List profile to reflect the contest result

**The Black List Platform Strategy:**
- Upload your script to blcklst.com
- Get 2-3 professional evaluations ($75 each)
- If you score 8+ out of 10, your script surfaces to managers and producers who actively browse the platform
- It's not a contest, but high scores generate inbound requests

**WGA Registration:**
- $20 for non-WGA members
- Do this before submitting anywhere, every time
- Not a copyright substitute (copyright your script too — it's worth it), but establishes a clear date of creation
- Register at wga.org/register
