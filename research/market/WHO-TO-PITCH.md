# Who to Pitch

Managers, production companies, streaming programs, and fellowships that accept material from unknown writers.

**Important:** Submission policies change constantly. Verify current status before submitting. Never send a full script without request.

---

## Literary Managers Who Accept Queries

These companies are known to accept cold queries and have signed debut writers in recent years. Research each one before querying — understand their clients and what they've recently sold.

### Tier 1 (Most Active)

**Kaplan/Perrone Entertainment**
Actively builds careers with new writers. Strong track record with genre material and elevated drama. Submit via email. Query only, synopsis required.

**Grandview**
Known for signing fresh talent from contests and fellowships. Strong connections at streaming. Email query with logline and genre.

**Madhouse Entertainment**
Represents a strong roster of TV and film writers. Open to cold queries with compelling loglines in genre space.

**Writ Large**
Smaller company that takes on newer writers. Very selective, but known for careful career development. Email query.

**The Gersh Agency (junior lit agents)**
Gersh's junior agents are worth approaching, particularly if you have contest credentials or a produced short.

**Circle of Confusion**
Represents genre writers heavily. Known for horror, sci-fi, and action. Open submission portal on their site.

**Zero Gravity Management**
Strong in commercial genre, action, thriller. Have signed writers with zero credits. Worth querying if your material is commercial.

**Benderspink**
One of the more production-friendly management companies. They also produce, which means if they sign you, they might attach themselves to produce your script. Email query.

### Finding More

**The most reliable method:** Go to IMDbPro. Find a film that's in the same genre/tone as your script. Find the screenwriter. Find the screenwriter's manager (under "representation"). Add them to your list.

This approach finds managers who have already demonstrated taste aligned with your material.

## Production Companies With Open Portals or Known Open Submissions

### Major Companies

**Blumhouse Productions**
blumhouse.com/talent
The most accessible major production company for unknown genre writers. They have a formal open submission process. Focus: horror and thriller under $6M. If your script fits their model (contained, high-concept, scalable horror), this is your best shot at a major company reading cold material.

Requirements: logline + 1-page synopsis first. Full script on request.

**Skydance Media**
skydance.com
Accepts pitches through their development portal. Big on action, sci-fi, and elevated thriller. More receptive to writers with some traction (contest placements, etc.) but not closed to new voices.

**eOne**
entertainmentone.com
International production and distribution. Active in horror, thriller, and genre. Accepts queries through management or directly via their development slate contacts.

**Automatik Entertainment**
Produces critically acclaimed indie films. Known for elevated genre and character-driven thriller. Their development team reads broadly. Best approach is email query with logline.

**3311 Productions**
Genre-focused, known for finding spec material. Active buyers.

**Topic Studios**
Prestige and political drama. Part of First Look Media. Active development team.

**Orion Pictures (MGM subsidiary)**
Has historically been open to spec horror and genre material through their genre label.

### Horror-Specific

**Epix Entertainment / MGM Television** — Active in limited series thriller.
**Shudder Originals** — Shudder (AMC Networks) produces horror originals with smaller budgets. They work through established producers but are worth understanding as a final home.
**Alcon Entertainment** — Genre films, known for accessible development process.

## Streaming Writing Programs

These programs are the fastest legitimate path to a professional writing career. Many have launched careers from zero.

### Film-Focused

**The Black List**
blcklst.com

The industry's script marketplace. Upload your script. Industry readers evaluate it. High scores get exposure to managers and producers. Coverage system.

**Annual Black List:** Each December, Black List staff release a curated list of the most-liked unproduced scripts. Being on the annual Black List is career-making.

**Cost:** $35/month to host. $30 per evaluation. Worth it if your script is truly ready.

## Writing Fellowships and Programs

### Disney / ABC Writing Program
**disneystudiosprogrammes.com**

One of the most competitive and valuable programs in television. 1-year paid fellowship ($50K+/year). You're assigned as a staff writer on a Disney-owned TV show.

Open to: emerging TV writers. Application requires: writing sample (spec episode or original pilot), application, interview.

Deadline: typically late spring/early summer. Check site for current cycle.

Being a Disney/ABC alumni opens virtually every door in television.

### CBS Diversity Writing Initiative
**cbstvstudios.com/programs**

CBS's diversity-focused program. Pairs writers with shows from CBS Studios' portfolio. Paid position. Primarily for TV writers.

Application: spec script (episode of an existing show) plus original pilot. Competitive.

### NBC Writers On The Verge
**nbcupap.com**

NBC's 6-month program for emerging TV writers. Intensive training + networking. Places writers on NBC shows.

Particularly strong for comedy writers. Application includes writing sample + video essay.

### Warner Bros. Television Writing Workshop (now the WB Workshop)
Annual program focused on drama and comedy. Competitive. Strong alumni network throughout the WB television ecosystem.

### Sundance Feature Lab
**sundance.org**

Not a fellowship — a labatory program for feature films in development. If you're also a director or attached to a director, this is worth pursuing. Two programs: Screenwriters Lab (early development) and Directors Lab.

Application requires: project in development + letter of recommendation.

Sundance Lab alumni make up a significant portion of the independent American cinema landscape.

### The Nicholl Fellowship (Academy Awards)
See `CONTESTS-AND-FELLOWSHIPS.md`. The most prestigious screenplay contest in the world. Finalists often get representation immediately.

## How to Find Contacts via IMDbPro

IMDbPro ($20/month) is essential. Here's the specific workflow:

1. **Find comp films** — movies in your genre, tone, and budget range from the past 3-5 years.

2. **Find the writer** — go to the film's page, click the writers.

3. **Check the writer's rep** — under their profile, find "agent" and "manager."

4. **Research the rep** — go to the manager/agent's company page. See who else they rep. Understand their taste.

5. **Note contact info** — IMDbPro often has contact emails or agency addresses.

6. **Query with specificity** — mention one of their existing clients in your query: "I noticed you represent [Writer X], whose work on [Film Y] is a clear comp for my material."

This personal touch significantly improves response rates. It tells the manager you've done your research and that you're not spamming a generic list.

## The Stage 32 Approach

Stage 32 (stage32.com) is a filmmaker network that also operates a pitch service.

**Happy Writers Pitchfests:** Pitch your project in a 3-5 minute video to producers, managers, and development executives who have opted in. These are actual industry professionals, not predatory vanity services.

Cost: $40-$80 per pitch.

The best Stage 32 connections are with working producers at smaller production companies who are actively seeking material. Not your path to a studio deal, but potentially your path to a first option and a produced credit.

## The Black List Approach (Detailed)

**Step 1:** Your script must be truly ready. Get at least three trusted reads from informed readers before uploading. If you can't get positive feedback from three smart readers, don't upload.

**Step 2:** Upload your script. Write a strong synopsis. This is marketing copy — spend real time on it.

**Step 3:** Purchase at least 2-3 evaluations. A score of 7-8 out of 10 is good. An 8+ score gets significant industry attention. Under 6 and you'll need to rewrite and resubmit.

**Step 4:** If you're getting consistent high scores, industry members will find you organically. You can also pitch your script directly to specific managers/producers through the platform.

**The realistic picture:** The Black List works for scripts that are genuinely excellent and commercially positioned. It's not a lottery — it's a quality filter. If your script is good, it works. If it needs another draft, you'll know.

## Platform Landscape Update (2025)

### Coverfly — SHUT DOWN August 2025
Coverfly closed operations in August 2025, along with WeScreenplay and ScreenCraft (same parent company). If you see references to Coverfly anywhere, they're outdated. The platform no longer exists.

### FilmFreeway (filmfreeway.com) — Still Active, Use With Caution
FilmFreeway remains the largest aggregator of contests and festivals. It's the easiest way to find submission opportunities in one place.

**The caveat:** FilmFreeway does not vet the legitimacy of every festival or contest on their platform. A significant percentage of listed "competitions" are low-value or outright predatory — collecting fees, offering no meaningful prize or industry exposure, sometimes run by a single person with no industry connections.

**How to use FilmFreeway safely:**
- Only submit to contests with verifiable track records (previous winners who went on to industry work)
- Research the organizers before paying
- Cross-reference against the Tier 1/2 list in `CONTESTS-AND-FELLOWSHIPS.md`
- Avoid any contest promising "guaranteed distribution" or "producer meetings" as an entry benefit

### Greenlightmymovie (greenlightmymovie.com)
Newer platform for pitching directly to production executives. Video pitch format (3-5 minutes). Some legitimate producers participate. Worth monitoring but still establishing its track record.

### Kinolime (blog.kinolime.com)
Not a submission platform — a solid blog covering the current spec market, submission strategy, and industry news from a writer's perspective. Useful for staying current.
